LIGHT OF HIS FAMILY

TO HIS FAMILY, HE WAS BANKU.

IT WAS THE NAME THEY CALLED HIM AT HOME, THE NAME THAT HELD THEIR LOVE. SHREEYAM CHAULAGAIN WAS A FRIENDLY AND MATURE KIND OF SON WHO UNDERSTOOD THE WORLD MORE THAN HIS 17 YEARS WOULD SUGGEST.

HE WAS A YOUNG MAN JUST BEGINNING TO BUILD HIMSELF. AFTER FINISHING 10TH GRADE, HE FOUND A PASSION FOR THE GYM, SHAPING HIS BODY WITH THE SAME DISCIPLINE HE APPLIED TO EVERYTHING ELSE.

HIS DREAMS AND LOVE

HE LOVED FOOTBALL AND BASKETBALL. WHILE STUDYING IN GRADE 12 AT GLOBAL COLLEGE IN KATHMANDU, HE WAS ALREADY TEACHING HIMSELF GERMAN AT HOME, DREAMING OF A FUTURE IN GERMANY.

HE GREW UP IN HIS MAMAGHAR IN ITAHARI, SURROUNDED BY COUSINS WHO WERE MORE LIKE HIS SIBLINGS. HE WAS THE GLUE THAT HELD THEM TOGETHER.

HE THEN MOVED TO KATHMANDU FOR HIS 11TH AND 12TH. IN KATHMANDU, HE LIVED WITH HIS PARENTS, HIS SISTER LIVED ABROAD AND HIS HEART BELONGED TO HIS DOG, JOJO.

A MARTYR'S RESOLVE

WHEN THE PROTESTS BEGAN, HIS FAMILY BEGGED HIM NOT TO GO. "JUST WATCH TODAY, YOU CAN GO TOMORROW," THEY TOLD HIM.

"IF I BECOME A MARTYR FIGHTING FOR MY COUNTRY, TO END CORRUPTION, THEN SO BE IT," HE SAID TO THEM.

ON 8TH SEPTEMBER, HE WENT TO THE PROTEST IN HIS COLLEGE UNIFORM, DIRECTLY AFTER HIS CLASSES ENDED.
